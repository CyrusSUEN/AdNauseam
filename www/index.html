[Ad Nauseam]

Created by: Daniel C. Howe, Helen Nissenbaum

Download [Ad Nauseam] for Firefox

Background

[Ad Nauseam] is a lightweight browser extension that helps protect users against surveillance and data-profiling by online advertisers and ad networks. It does so not by means of concealment and encryption (i.e. covering one's tracks), but instead, paradoxically, by the opposite strategy: noise and obfuscation. [Ad Nauseam] works silently in the background of your web browser by clicking all the ads on a page, thereby obscuring user interests in a cloud of decoy clicks. In light of the industry's failure to achieve consensus on a Do Not Track standard, [Ad Nauseam] allows individual users to take matters into their own hands, offering cover against certain forms of surveillance, profiling, and practices of discrimination.

For the past 1.5 years W3C has led an effort which has engaged NGOs, industry reps from advertising and tech attempting to achieve a consensus on a standard for Do Not Track (DNT). From several trusted sources, the ad industry continuously sabotages progress, often at the 11th hour, so much so that at least two of the chairs have stepped down in frustration. A successful outcome would allow individuals to convey to websites that they don't want to be tracked (e.g. similar to a robots.txt file for server operators). [Ad Nauseam] represents a similar approach to the TrackMeNot (http://cs.nyu.edu/trackmenot) project, which attempts to relocate power on the hands of individual users, rather then vast commercial search/advertising corporations. For further information on this approach, please see: 'TrackMeNot: Resisting Surveillance in Web Search' (http://cs.nyu.edu/trackmenot/TMN-Howe-Niss08-ch23.pdf).
